About Us

A centre devoted to the incarnational gospel.

The Ezra Centre was born out of a simple conviction: that the kingdom of God must be built upon the foundation of Christ — with the right principle, and the right pattern, that he himself has shown us.

Our Story

Why a centre named for Ezra?

Ezra the priest, empowered by the Spirit of Christ, modelled a pattern that was later personified in Christ himself. Scripture records it in a single, loaded sentence:

"For Ezra had prepared and set his heart to seek the Law of the Lord, and to do and teach in Israel its statutes and its ordinances."

Note the order: prepared, set his heart, seek, do, and only then teach. This is not a footnote — it is the divine order of every sustainable ministry, and yet it is the order most often reversed in the Church today.

Incarnation as pattern

The fleshing out of God's living Word as a lifestyle is one of the most distinguishing elements of Christianity. It is reflected in the very etymology of the term Christian: from "the Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us" (John 1:14) to "it was in Antioch that the followers of Christ were first called Christians" (Acts 11:26).

The outward expression of Christ's character in the lives of his disciples — as a definite work of redeeming grace — has its fountainhead in our spirit, or heart, the core of our being. What does not take its root from such depth is neither sustainable nor can it be characteristic of us as Christ-ians.

Our conviction

All religions hold out as an ideal love, goodness, and holiness — but it is only the Christian faith that is able to produce it in real life experience. The Word is alive and powerful; on entering the heart it reconfigures the inner man, the core of our being, from which flows our doing.

This is our conviction: that practical, behavioral conduct should precede and be prioritized over our teaching, preaching, and evangelism. It is this imperative that makes the gospel message life-giving and life-changing.

Leadership

Those who lead the work.

The Ezra Centre is led by a couple whose lives together embody the very pattern the ministry exists to proclaim — being, doing, and only then teaching.

Founders

The Founders

Servants of the incarnational gospel

For decades, the founders of The Ezra Centre have walked the path of discipleship — not as performers on a public stage, but as witnesses whose lives together have been shaped, corrected, and renewed by the Word they now teach.

Their partnership is itself part of the message: that the fruit of the Spirit is grown in the soil of long obedience, shared trials, and a home ordered around the Lord. What they teach flows from what they have lived.

The Ezra Centre stands as the extension of that shared life — a vessel through which they now equip other saints to walk the same ancient, incarnational path.

Our Values

What shapes our work.

Four convictions that govern every program, every teaching, every relationship.

i.

Scripture, prayerfully

We do not innovate the gospel. We seek it in the Scriptures, submit to it in prayer, and let it reform us before we proclaim it.

ii.

Formation over performance

The measure of ministry is not platform or reach, but whether Christ is being formed in the saints. We labor for depth, not visibility.

iii.

Fellowship and accountability

Transformation does not happen in isolation. We nurture relationships of honest confession, mutual correction, and patient love.

iv.

Long obedience

The kingdom advances not through crisis but through continuity — the faithful, daily walk with Christ, year after year.
